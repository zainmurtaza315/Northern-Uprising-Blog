Raptors Expectations Higher Than Ever!

Posted by Zain Murtaza on January 3rd, 2016

The Raptors president and GM Masai Ujiri saw his team swirling into a 7-12 start in the early stage of the 2013-2014 season and decided he had seen enough. Rudy Gay was quickly traded, to much of the delight of a lot of Raptor fans and sports radio hosts. The Raptors since then have not looked back. Although falling short of their post season dreams in both campaigns after the Rudy Gay trade, the Raps post an astonishing regular season record of 103-64, the best record in the Eastern Conference in that span and the 6th best in the league. Much has gone on since the high volume scorer has left Toronto. The Raps have seen their two core players, develop into perennial All Stars and combine to be one of the most dynamic backcourts the league has to offer. Both Lowry and DeRozan have exceeded expectations and propelled the Raptors to unprecedented heights as a franchise. If you are looking for one more statistic to be further convinced how much this team has transformed from the gut wrenching days of Gary Forbes, Sonny Weems and Andrea Bargnani being recognized as notable Raptors, the Raptors were last below .500 on January 8th of 2014. Only Golden State (NBA Champions), Chicago and Toronto have been .500 or over every day for the past two calendar years. Good enough for ya? We thought so.
